Greenhouse Gases

This graph shows a rapid increase in greenhouse gases and, if you don't live under a rock, you have probably heard people talking about the increase in greenhouse gases. But, what exactly does this mean? Greenhouse gases are gases that trap heat within the atmosphere. Think about it this way - sunlight and heat can pass through the glass window of a car, but can't escape. Greenhouse gases are pretty much the same. Recently, greenhouse gas levels have been rising to dangerously high levels, causing what is known as global warming, where the Earth's climate is constantly getting hotter.

The Cause

The main cause for the increase in greenhouse gas emissions is energy production. It accounts for about 34% of greenhouse gases in the atmosphere. These are the steps to produce energy.

Mining

This is the first step in getting energy to homes, businesses, etc. We have to mine for fossil fuel, coal, natural gas, uranium, etc. This releases methane and carbon dioxide.

Refining

This is converting the raw energy into something that can be used. For example, crude oil is converted into to petroleum, also known as gasoline. This releases carbon dioxide and nitrous oxide.

Burning

This is where the energy is actually produced. The fossil fuel or natural gas is burned. This releases carbon dioxide, methane, and nitrous oxide.

Transportation

This is the final part, when the energy is transported through power cables and delivered into power outlets. This sometimes releases carbon dioxide.
